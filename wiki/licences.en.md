# Licensing

**RetroBat remains free software.** APIExpose is an independent plugin, licensed separately — it does not make RetroBat a paid product.

## Personal use

**Personal, educational, community and non-commercial** use is free. One personal machine = one personal APIExpose license.

## Commercial use

Any commercial use requires a **written license**: arcade cabinets, mini-PCs, preconfigured hard drives, SD cards or USB drives, digital packs, preconfigured RetroBat distributions, paid installation or support services — and more generally any paid offer where APIExpose is included, advertised, configured or used.

```text
1 machine sold = 1 APIExpose reseller license
```

A reseller may not copy a personal license to multiple machines, nor hide APIExpose license information.

## The Data Pack

The data files — XML, `.MEM` files, mappings, RAM definitions, outputs, panels, profiles and metadata — form the **APIExpose Data Pack**, protected by a dedicated license (`DATA-LICENSE.md`). It ships with the releases for personal use; its commercial redistribution follows the same rules as the plugin.

## The full texts

In the repository and in every release archive:

- `LICENSE.md` — the general license;
- `PERSONAL-LICENSE.md` — personal use;
- `COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md` — commercial and reseller use;
- `DATA-LICENSE.md` — the Data Pack;
- `NOTICE.md` — the reseller notice.

Bundled third-party tools (ffmpeg, ImageMagick, translateLocally) remain under their respective licenses.
